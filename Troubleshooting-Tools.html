Tips and Tricks for Troubleshooting and staying safe!

  • If you want to know your Internet speeds click 'Speed Test'. For more details make to to click 'Show more info' after the speed test is done. Speed Test
    1. The download lets you know how many speeds you are getting. Mbps so make sure you know what you are paying for from your Internet Service Provider so you know if you are getting slow speeds or not.
    2. Once you see the Download finish click, (Show more info) there you will see your Upload. You don`t have to worry too much about that unless you are streaming videos or a streamer for gaming.
    3. You want to make sure your ping is low,(On Fast.com it is called Loaded). The lower the better 20 ms is exceptional, between 50 ms and 100 ms is average, but anything above 150 ms is not that good.
    4. Client lets you know where you are located at and gives you your IP (make sure not to give that away!) and Server(s) lets you know where your service is coming from.
  • If you want to see real life cyber attacks click 'Bitdefender Cyberthreat Real-Time Map'. Also, With Bitdefender, you get the most innovative technologies that predict, prevent, detect and remediate even the latest cyber-threats, anywhere in the world. Bitdefender Cyberthreat Real-Time Map
    1. Legend lets you know what kind of attacks are going on right now. As you can see they are color-coded.
    2. Live Attacks show you the time, attack, attack type, attack country, and target country.
    3. Locations show you what places have been attacked recently.
  • If you are worried about cyber attacks and feel like you are not safe, Click here to learn more about Bitdefender! Bitdefender
  • MAC Address Lookup click here! WireShark
    1. If you ever look into your routers firewall log and not sure what MAC Address is showing you can always use WireShark.
    2. Just copy and past the first 6 characters and past them in the OUI Search box and click find. At the bottom, it will say Results and it will kind of give you a hint on what it might be. You can always copy and past that results on google.com and search up the device information as well.
